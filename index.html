Chapter Summaries

Chapter 1: A Cushy Job?

Roy Follows describes his early experiences in the Malay Police Force during the Malayan Emergency. He recounts his initial posting to Pasir Kemudi, his struggles with the language and jungle warfare, and his eventual transfer to a jungle company.

Chapter 2: From a View to Kill

Follows details his experiences with the 10th Police Jungle Company, including a successful ambush and the challenges of jungle patrolling. He also describes the camaraderie and the diverse backgrounds of his platoon members.

Chapter 3: You are Trapped and Outnumbered - Surrender!

This chapter recounts a harrowing experience where Follows and his platoon were trapped and outnumbered by Communist terrorists. Despite being low on supplies and outnumbered, they managed to survive and inflict casualties on the enemy.

Chapter 4: Hand to Hand

Follows describes a close combat encounter with a Communist terrorist. He details the brutal hand-to-hand fight and the subsequent capture of the terrorist, Ng Aik Peng.

Chapter 5: Dead Fish and Frog-Piss

This chapter covers various incidents, including an ambush gone wrong due to a monitor lizard, the challenges of jungle life, and the impact of the environment on the soldiers' health and morale.

Chapter 6: Brotherly Love - and a Nasty Headache

Follows recounts an operation involving a turncoat named Lam, who betrayed his own brother. The chapter also introduces Inspector Chan of the CID and describes the preparations for a jungle patrol.

Chapter 7: Fiascos 1 and 2

This chapter details two failed operations and the lessons learned from these experiences.

Chapter 8: Of Ants and Elephants

Follows describes encounters with wildlife in the jungle, including ants, elephants, and the challenges posed by these animals.

Chapter 9: Among the Aborigines: the Trouble with Pangoi

This chapter covers Follows' experiences with the aboriginal tribes in central Malaya and the challenges of working with them.

Chapter 10: Whose Ambush?

Follows recounts an ambush operation and the uncertainties and dangers involved in such missions.

Chapter 11: Goodbye to Fort Brooke

This chapter describes the transition and changes in the Police Field Force, including the move from Fort Brooke.

Chapter 12: 'What Harry Sees, Harry Hits' - Sometimes

Follows details an operation involving aerial support and the challenges of coordinating with aircraft.

Chapter 13: The Trap

This chapter recounts a successful operation where Follows and his platoon set a trap for Communist terrorists.

Chapter 14: The Nasty Surprise Department

Follows describes various surprises and challenges encountered during jungle operations.

Epilogue

The epilogue reflects on the experiences and the impact of the Malayan Emergency on Follows and his comrades.
